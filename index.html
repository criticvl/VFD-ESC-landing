About the Project

About the Project

The main source of mechanical power in most modern day industrial applications is three phase induction motors. While having many benefits such as being economical, efficient, reliable, it has its limitations _ the speed and torque is not adjustable, it is constant. Because of this flaw, three phase induction motors are unusable for applications that need mechanical speed lower than its rated maximum.

For this reason our team decided to build Variable Frequency Drive (Electronic Speed Control). This device is directly connected to motor and is designed to vary its speed by outputting three phase voltage at different frequencies. VFDs offer increased energy efficiency, improved control, thus making already important induction motors much more powerful.

Hardware Components

  • STM32F103R8 microcontroller unit _ comes with enough speed and memory, has lots of general purpose input-output (GPIO) and peripherals, provides multiple timers with enough PWM outputs making it perfect MCU for out project.

  • IRFP460 N channel mosfets and IR2110 high voltage gate drivers _ these components were essential for handling high voltage and power of our prototype.

  • MAX485 IC _ we used this device for external communication, it converts microcontroller’s UART output into one that’s compatible with RS485.

Software Components

Used for MCU firmware _ microcontroller peripheral configuration, generation of PWM outputs

Used Altium Designer for building PCB design and schematic
